## Super-Resolution

- **Wang et al. (2018)** — "ESRGAN: Enhanced Super-Resolution Generative Adversarial Networks," *ECCV 2018 Workshops*. Introduced RRDB architecture (basis for our RRDBNet6x), perceptual loss using VGG features before activation, network interpolation for perception-distortion trade-off. Demonstrates that perceptual/adversarial loss achieves lower PSNR but better visual quality than pixel-loss-only models. https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_ECCVW_2018/papers/11133/Wang_ESRGAN_Enhanced_Super-Resolution_Generative_Adversarial_Networks_ECCVW_2018_paper.pdf

### Perception-Distortion Trade-off

- **Blau & Michaeli (2018)** — "The Perception-Distortion Tradeoff," *CVPR 2018*. Mathematically proves that distortion metrics (PSNR, RMSE) and perceptual quality cannot be simultaneously optimized — improving one necessarily worsens the other. Provides theoretical justification for why perceptual loss models achieve lower PSNR but better spatial detail. https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.06077

- **Jo et al. (2020)** — "Investigating Loss Functions for Extreme Super-Resolution," *CVPR 2020 Workshops*. Empirical study of loss function combinations for SR. Confirms perception-distortion trade-off in practice: pixel loss maximizes PSNR, perceptual loss improves visual quality at cost of PSNR. https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_CVPRW_2020/papers/w31/Jo_Investigating_Loss_Functions_for_Extreme_Super-Resolution_CVPRW_2020_paper.pdf

### Domain Gap: Synthetic vs Real Degradation (for Introduction)

These papers document that SR models trained on bicubic/synthetic degradation fail on real satellite imagery. They are NOT direct competitors — they still use synthetic data (just more realistic). None do hyperspectral or EMIT-specific SR.

- **Dong et al. (2022)** — "Real-world remote sensing image super-resolution via a practical degradation model and a kernel-aware network," *ISPRS J. Photogrammetry & Remote Sensing*, 191, 155–170. Proposes realistic degradation model (real blur kernels + noise) instead of bicubic. RGB/multispectral RS, not hyperspectral. Key quote for citing: "existing methods fail in many real-world scenarios because they are trained on synthetic data generated by a single degradation model." https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0924271622001824

- **Zhang et al. (2022)** — "Single-Image Super Resolution of Remote Sensing Images with Real-World Degradation Modeling," *Remote Sensing*, 14(12), 2895. Same concept: more realistic degradation for RS-SR. Also RGB/multispectral. https://www.mdpi.com/2072-4292/14/12/2895

- **Rad et al. (2021)** — "Benefiting from Bicubically Down-Sampled Images for Learning Real-World Image Super-Resolution," *WACV 2021*. General CV paper (not RS). Documents domain gap between bicubic-trained models and real images. https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.03053

### Cross-Sensor Real Training Data (closest conceptual relative)

- **SEN2NAIP (2024)** — "SEN2NAIP: A large-scale dataset for Sentinel-2 Image Super-Resolution," *Scientific Data*. Cross-sensor pairs: S2 (10m) + NAIP aerial (2.5m). Learns degradation model between sensors. MOST SIMILAR to our approach conceptually, but multispectral not hyperspectral. 2,851 image pairs, 4× SR. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-024-04214-y

### Hyperspectral SHSR Reviews

- **Chen et al. (2023)** — "A Review of Hyperspectral Image Super-Resolution Based on Deep Learning," *Remote Sensing*, 15, 2853. Comprehensive DL-based HSI SR review. Standard problem formulation (Eqs. 1-5). Notes MAE is most common loss. All reviewed methods train on synthetic data (CAVE, Harvard, Pavia). https://www.mdpi.com/2072-4292/15/11/2853

- **Jiang et al. (2020)** — "Learning Spatial-Spectral Prior for Super-Resolution of Hyperspectral Imagery," *IEEE Trans. Computational Imaging*. SSPSR network. Uses synthetic degradation on standard HSI benchmarks. https://github.com/junjun-jiang/SSPSR

### Key Novelty Argument

No existing work uses real cross-sensor fusion (HSI+MSI) to generate training targets for hyperspectral SHSR. Existing "real-world SR" papers improve the degradation model but still synthesize pairs. SEN2NAIP is the closest concept but is multispectral only. Our work is the first to apply this to EMIT hyperspectral data.
